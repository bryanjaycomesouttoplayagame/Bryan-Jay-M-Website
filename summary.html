Trust the Process

When you're trying to achieve something, determination is innate to the core. You always got what it takes to be the best. Where doubts disguises as a hindrance that limits the hidden potential. If only I could buy more time to explain the step by step process with this one. I mean, it's pretty obvious it has something to do with artificial intelligence, youtube tutorials and online articles. Well, not everything. I just simply use it as a guide and some techniques doesn't work, improvisation does. The machine learning is learning because I figure out it has flaws.

Designing a webpage is kind of hassle for a phone. Showcasing the skills, projects and accomplishments. Narrowing it down, the process of making a website and how to make it responsive. This specific tasks I'm counting on it'll be a better solid explanation later on. When it comes with creativity I'll definitely slay it. To get started, first, I sketch the layout of the web interface on how is going to look like. Following (some) of the instructions. Starting with studying the proper format of a hypertext markup language. Utilizing the cascading stylesheets, for the proper modification and specificity. Then choosing a color palette scheme. Simply put as shades of green, orange and brown. I choose an ecru for the background of the body. This is the least used in web design. Sure, it might look bland and stale. It is unappreciated but still this is the best bet for me. I don't want the website to be user-friendly, colorful or dull just neutral. I tried to encorporate the complementary colors like tangerine and cream as a customized text-background for the footer, mixing vivid color such as darker olive slab and darker shades of brown.


After that, I put some finishing touches. I conclude an additional voiceover for a user's covenient experience, adding some subtle icon animation embedded to font awesome and a transparent hovering effect on the link. Sliders that hides the navigation links such as home, projects, summary, about and contacts (also known as footer) in the background. A search box that redirects to Google and customizing a placeholder. One thing I'm really curious to implement is the toggle and switch, the dim and the bright mode. When I look at the cork board at the dorm I think this is it. Although I try to put a grain texture video as a site background, well... the result, it doesn't look like a site at all. I didn't want animation effects on the body as a background. It's kind of distracting to the reader. I want my personal website to be simple yet visually appealing and stunning on its own.
